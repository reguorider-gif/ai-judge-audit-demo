Why this exists

A score tells you what happened. It rarely tells you what to do next.

LLM-as-judge workflows are useful, but product teams still need an artifact they can inspect, challenge, and attach to a review process. AI Judge turns the ambiguous middle into a concrete handoff.

The eval failed. Why? Was the answer unsupported, contradicted, incomplete, or just phrased too confidently?
The judge disagreed. Where? Which seats noticed the source conflict, and which ones only scored the final answer?
A reviewer needs to sign off. On what? A plain score is not enough for support, compliance, regression, or research review.

AI Judge creates the missing review artifact.

It decomposes the answer into claims, binds each claim to explicit source fragments, summarizes judge agreement, and writes the final verdict in a format a human can read without opening the whole trace stack.
