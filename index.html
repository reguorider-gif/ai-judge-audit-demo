Why this exists

A score tells you what happened. A packet tells you what to do next.

LLM-as-judge workflows are useful, but product teams still need an artifact they can inspect, challenge, and attach to a review process.

The eval failed. Why? Was the answer unsupported, contradicted, incomplete, or phrased too confidently?
The judge disagreed. Where? Which seats noticed the source conflict, and which ones only scored the final answer?
A reviewer needs to sign off. On what? A plain score is not enough for support, compliance, regression, or research review.
The missing handoff

AI Judge creates the review artifact after the eval.

It decomposes the answer into claims, binds each claim to source fragments, summarizes judge agreement, and writes the final verdict in a format a human can read without opening the full trace stack.
